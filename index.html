Wacom tablets, revived.

MockTab is a native macOS driver that brings discontinued Wacom drawing tablets back to life on Apple Silicon and Intel Macs. Full pressure, tilt, and rotation — without the bloat of the official driver.

Why MockTab

Wacom makes excellent hardware, but old products eventually fall off the support list. Reliable, perfectly healthy tablets stop working with newer macOS releases. MockTab is a small, focused driver that targets exactly that gap: USB and Bluetooth Wacom tablets from 1996 through the late 2010s, on macOS 13 and newer.

Tablet area mapping

Choose which part of the tablet maps to which part of the screen. Proportional mode keeps circles round on any aspect ratio, so the pen feels like the pen.

Pressure curve

A two-point Bézier editor with Linear, Soft, and Firm presets. Works in every pressure-aware app — Photoshop, Affinity, Krita, Procreate Dreams, Rebelle, Clip Studio.

Buttons & express keys

Remap barrel buttons, touch ring, and express keys to any modifier-plus-key combination. Live capture: click the field, press the shortcut, done.

Display mapping

Route the tablet to any connected display. Useful for multi-monitor setups where you want the pen confined to a single screen.

Live scratchpad

Test pressure, tilt, and button mappings in a built-in scratchpad before you open your real work.

What's in the box

  • Pressure, tilt, and pen rotation (where the hardware supports it)
  • Wireless via USB dongle and Bluetooth
  • Multiple tablets connected simultaneously — pressure-based switching
  • Per-app settings for area, pressure, and buttons
  • Touch ring and touch strip
  • Native AppKit app, signed and notarized, no kernel extensions

What it isn't

  • A driver for Huion, XP-Pen, Xencelabs, or other vendors
  • Support for new (post-2020) Wacom tablets
  • Available for Windows, Linux, or iPad
  • A touch / gesture driver (touch is detected but disabled)
